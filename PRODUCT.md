# Background

The amount of effort that goes into women's wellbeing is often undermined. The world is seeing a wave of hypermonitoring health through wearables & other diagnostic methods. However, still a lot of these frameworks & methods are either generalised or designed for men. Moreover, a lot of generalised knowledge tends to be biased towards men because the research was primarily done on men. As a result, women end up trying to fit into the same design which is not even suitable for them in the first place. As we see the lives getting more fast-paced and more women than ever entering the workforce, we are also seeing large numbers of women becoming endangered to issues like PCOS/PCOD, Endometrial cancer, Breast cancer, Obesity, Diabetes, Depression, Miscarriages, etc. A lot of these if not all can be avoided if women can take care of their bodies in a more informed manner. This starts with acknowledging that male & female bodies function in a largely different manner. It's even more different for women with hormonal disorders like PCOS (which are more common than ever, thanks to our lifestyles). Few examples:

- While running is generally fine for men, for women, it depends on what phase of the menstrual cycle they are in.
- Women experience higher cortisol response to fasted, high-intensity workouts
- For women, caloric intake requires an increase of 100-300 kcal during the luteal phase. Carbohydrate intake must increase pre-workout during the luteal phase to offset the body's shift toward fat oxidation. While for men, daily caloric and macronutrient targets remain static. Fasting windows (16:8) can be executed daily.
- For women with PCOS, weight loss is resistant to simple caloric deficits. They also need higher protein/fiber ratios to manage insulin resistance.

Compared to male biology, there are a lot more interdependencies & complexities in females. Hence, it becomes important to build a product to help women not only track but also take meaningful insight-based actions to cater to the complex clockwork of their body.


# Current problems

- It is generally easy to track the cycle for women but not everyone is aware of how their bodies function in each of the phase. Hence, they can't make the best out of it.
- The products available generally track individual aspects like sleep, energy, cycles, etc. well through different apps but still there's a lack of actionable insights & education about them.
- Apps like flo, cycles & glo are very specific to reproductive health. All they provide is a way to log things, which can be done with a spreadsheet too. There's a lot more to learn from your cycles apart from just "chances of pregnancy".
- Healthifyme & other calorie-tracker/weight-management apps miss the point really bad. They just ask you your current height-weight & your target weight; boom here's how many calories you should have daily, same formula for everyone. What about other measurements? What about my metabolism & gut health? For a person with PCOS who currently takes ~1800 calories, it recommends to start taking 1400 calories. That's not a valuable advise, it can cause cortisol, insulin, energy issues & also period delays.
- More advanced apps like whoop also provide information about sleep, movement & recovery but they are barely actionable unless you go & provide all the screenshots to chatgpt.
- People often choose to discuss these things with general ai apps like claude & chatgpt. Regardless of the amount of context you provide, you will get some advice which may or may not work for you.
- Women with unstable hormones really face serious challenges pertaining to mood, productivity, fitness & overall health. Balancing so many variables is like driving a car with several clutches.
- Apps like apple health provide the ability to "log" things but logging each & every metric manually is a lot of effort
- Apple has rings that you can set manually at a daily level (based on vibes). This is simple consistency loop, that works for most men. How many calories I should burn is affected a lot by the amount of rest I got, what you ate, which phase you are in, your cortisol levels & a lot of subjective things
- Eg: My calorie app doesn't know that I am in luteal phase but it still asks me to stay in a 600 cal deficit. Apple watch shows me a 600 cal move goal even on my periods/sick days. My cycle tracking app doesn't know that I had a stressful month and this months periods might get delayed (PCOS/PCOD-specific).

A female health & wellbeing journal powered by ai-agents. It can track metrics across all the levers: Nutrition, Movement, Rest, Mind. # What it is

A very good tracker that has all your data in one place which also happens to have an AI assitant (paired with my girlfriend's learnings) that can help you listen to your body. With the help of context it can flag general patterns/irregularities like sleep timings, sugar intake, caffiene intake, etc and give suggestions. It's a proactive journal that makes use of the logs & helps me adapt.

Examples:

- If it knows that my gut is weak & I share the nutritional information or photo of a snack that can agitate the gut, it can just tell me to have it after a meal.
- If it knows that my periods get thrown off if I do heavy workout in follicular phase, it can warn me before hand.
- If it knows your period cramps are generally painful, it will set the movement target & calorie intake target accordingly

# What it's not

A replacement for a nutritionist or a fitness trainer or a doctor.
